Count of different ways to express N as the sum of 1, 3 and 4  
	Not a coin change problem. Since repetition is allowed. Eg: Sum = 4 and coins = 1, 3, 4 then
possible ways are :

a) 1+1+1+1
b) 1+3
c) 3+1
d) 4

Note that b) and c) are treated as different ways thus differing from standard coin change problem (where those 2 are treated as one way).
